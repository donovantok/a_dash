FINANCIAL INFORMATION

Most insurance plans in California cover abortion. If you do not have insurance that covers abortion, you may be eligible for Family PACT, a family planning insurance program for California residents. Visit https://familypact.org/ for more information. Note that in determining eligibility, your own income should be used rather than your parents’, and that out-of-state students attending school in California may qualify as residents. The Presumptive Eligibility for Pregnant Women program also grants Medi-Cal coverage for eligible patients. Your abortion care provider should be able to help you determine your insurance options.

LEGAL INFORMATION

California prohibits abortions at or after viability. Exceptions are made for life endangerment or serious health risk.

California does not prohibit abortion based on the woman's reason for seeking an abortion.

California does not prohibit specific methods of abortion.

Beginning on January 1, 2023, health centers at all California public schools will be required to provide medication abortion. This policy applies to both the UC and CSU systems.
